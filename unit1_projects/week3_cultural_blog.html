Sunday, May 11, 2014

How do you see Dev Bootcamp?

Based on the video, what's your take on the DBC experience?

The DBC experience really seems more like a high intense workshop. It sounds like an exciting atmosphere to be in where everyone has the chance to exchange ideas, learn new tricks and of course, learn to program.

What is your impression of DBC?

I can’t really say that I have a particular impression yet. I do however see that students and staff really get to have this opportunity for open communication to not just talk about the material, but our thoughts and feelings as well. How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I’ve never been a person to delve into communication with others. I’ve always been an observer and slowly become part of a conversation. I really like listening to other people; so generally, I like to ask people about their opinions, visions or goals.

Have your expectations of DBC changed? If so, how?

My expectations about DBC haven’t changed much. I know and understand it’s about how much we want to learn or get out of the program based on our thirst. However, part of me still has the mind-set to be that customer in the restaurant expecting an order.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I’m am so excited to get to DBC Chicago and meet everyone. I’m excited in engaging in experience and opportunity. That’s what I’m nervous about the most. Sometimes I feel great about code and terminology I’ve learned and then have a short conversation with someone that fully understands the assignment. I’m nervous I will fall onto coattails not progress like others.

What makes me really nervous of this kind of environment is that I’m worried I won’t bring my ‘A’ Game all the time. I feel as if there is a lot of pressure with what we should know prior to Phase 1. I’m worried that I’ve found something I like and won’t be good at it soon enough.
